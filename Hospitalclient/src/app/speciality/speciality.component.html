Treatment in Nerves

Brain is the most complex organ in our body with more than 150 billion neurons (brain cells) that serve so many functions such as memory, judgement, personality development, sensory feelings, motor movements, coordination of the body, etc. Our nervous system works involuntarily and hence is called Autonomic Nervous System. It controls functions such as breathing, pumping of the heart, blood pressure control, gastro-intestinal function, urinary function, etc. The spinal cord and nerves transmit signals from the brain to muscles and other organs for normal functioning of the body. When there is a problem with the brain and nervous system, depending on the site that is involved, some very important functions are impaired or lost.
